The idea of typing a few words and watching a whole business take shape in front of me while I chug a case of energy drinks is tempting. But it's not realistic. And even if I wanted to, I can't just set the AI loose to generate code and trust that it'll be 100% functional. Spoiler: it won't.

I don't want to be a vibe coder. I can't be a vibe coder. I feel the genuine need to review the code AI produces. And every time I sharpen my ability to write prompts and specify what Claude actually needs to do, the results really are interesting. Most of the time, this is a massive productivity gain. At least for me, working solo. I don't know what it's like on a team using AI, I've never been through that.

But the more I use AI tools for tasks that used to be part of my daily routine as a developer, the less I want to do those same tasks the traditional way. I'm just becoming AI-dependent. I write prompts to help me write other prompts. Are we starting to see a problem here?

I used to be able to write complex SQL queries. I wasn't fast, I wasn't an expert at it, but I could do it. Now I describe the tables the database has, or even lazier, I paste the Prisma schema into the chat, tell it what I want from that data and how it should behave. A tweak or two. And there it is, my perfect little query, ready to use.

I feel, I can't prove it, but I feel I'm becoming less intelligent, less capable. It's funny how someone can become less capable and more productive at the same time. Somewhere, that math doesn't add up. Maybe I'm missing something, maybe I'm studying and applying AI the wrong way. But regardless, I wouldn't go back, even if I could. I just hope I find the balance I need so I don't turn into a functional idiot in a few years.
